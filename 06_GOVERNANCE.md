# Crowd Development Governance

Essential Crisis Software (ECS) is built to be resilient infrastructure. A system meant to survive when institutions fail cannot itself depend on a single person or a single point of control. That principle shapes how the project is governed. This document is the base, the community will have final voice of it.

## The non-founder-founder model

ECS was started by **Karol Kwestarz-Maciejewski**, a systems architect who grew tired of waiting for someone else to build resilience infrastructure, and who drew the initial blueprints and documentation.

He is intentionally **not in charge**. The moment one person becomes essential to the project, you have already failed at building resilient infrastructure.

The founder's standing role:

- If his code / blueprints are wrong, fork it and do better / solve the problem.
- If architecture is wrong, say so publicly, discuss argument.
- He holds no veto — not even author's privilege. Only until project take momentum governance above normal Admin and credits mark.
- He votes the same as everyone else, and acts as a compromise-seeker, not a decision-maker.

The **Enhanced.World Foundation** (non-profit, registering in the EU in 2026) is intended to hold the long-term legal and stewardship role, with a mission to use technology for human survival and development while preventing harmful use. Karol pursues the commercial side through Locally4Me Group OÜ (Estonia).

## How decisions get made

For technical and contribution matters, see [`CONTRIBUTING.md`](CONTRIBUTING.md). For everything else, the working model is:
Coding, Analog and Non-Coding community:
1. An idea / issue / need is proposed in its dedicated channel.
2. Discussion runs for a minimum of 72 hours.
3. The community votes (reaction or formal poll).
4. The result is logged in the meta channel.
5. Volunteers implement it.

In case of disagreements and appellate - 4 nominees from Core Contributors - SnP - BB Teams - decides. 

This model is itself open to refinement by the community.

## Recognition

Contributors are credited:

- Permanently on GitHub.
- In monthly transparency reports.
- With a governance voice proportional to their contribution.

Your contribution is your reputation. Influence is weighted by validated work and community consensus, not by tenure or title.

## Participation

- **I have an idea.** Post it in the relevant channel and start a discussion.
- **I want to contribute code.** Open or claim an issue, submit a pull request, get reviewed. Attribution and a share of future commercial revenue follow.
- **I disagree with a decision or with the founder's approach.** Raise it publicly and debate it. If consensus says it should change, it changes — or you fork and build an alternative.
- **I want a governance voice or access to restricted modules.** Contribute code, documentation, or organisation. Your voice is weighted by the work done.

## Tiered security architecture

Some work is too sensitive to develop fully in the open before the risk landscape allows it. ECS uses tiered disclosure:

- **Public** — most functional modules. Full source available, community audit welcome.
- **Restricted** — some critical modules, solutions, and plugins. Architectural overview is public; full code is available to core / verified contributors under agreement. Request access: research@enhanced.world.
- **Classified** — masking and data-protection components, sensitive protocols. Developed by the founder and validated contributors; a proof-of-security is available on request; no publication date is set until the risk decreases. In-case / security related for Foundation Vault commissions.

Restricted work covers data protection, encryption, steganography, and related sub-modules and plugins. If you want to contribute there, **start from a GitHub Discussion** for guidance and validation during development, rather than submitting finished sensitive code cold.

Before a formal Security & Privacy team exists, the project consults outside experts as needed. The team is then formed from contributing experts in data privacy and security and code analysis, and may include people connected to the Enhanced.World Foundation once it launches.

## Roadmap of governance phases

- **Phase 0.** Open invitation to developers and co-creators; methodology and scenario discussion opens.
- **Phase 1.** Technical discussion, advising, validation, and recommendations on architecture and general blueprints.
- **Phase 2.** Validation, detailed plans, technology choices, and division of sub-projects: system node → general software → module → plugin.
- **Phases 3–4.** Core developers and experts begin critical elements and restricted modules.
- **Phases 5–6.** Testing and optimization (power, longevity, time, efficiency, security); extension of plugins, custom builds, hardware recommendations, and community resilience implementation.

## Open questions for the community

These need to be formalized together, and your input shapes them before momentum builds:

- Voting mechanisms — how do we make fair decisions?
- Contributor tiers — what earns more voice?
- Dispute resolution — what happens when people disagree?
- Succession — what happens if the founder is no longer able to participate?

If you want to help build infrastructure that is genuinely decentralized — not centralized control wearing decentralized clothing — you're welcome here.
