# Support the mission

Building resilient, privacy-focused, socially useful technology takes more than code. It takes people, partnerships, infrastructure, and long-term commitment. ECS is developed with a public-benefit perspective, alongside a foundation focused on resilience, ethics, and social impact.

This work currently runs alongside full-time employment. Additional support directly increases the time, development effort, and resources that can go into delivering these projects and expanding their real-world impact.

## The wider portfolio

ECS is one of several open initiatives looking for collaboration:

- **Essential Crisis Software (ECS)** — open-source, pro-bono crisis software: scenario-based alternative networks and utilities. *GitHub open.*
- **Essential Cybersecurity Benchmark (ECB)** — a digital-safety event generating research-grade behavioural and technical datasets. *Ready for participant invitations, testing, and validation.*
- **Food4Me / Food4Free** — a consumer–producer–services platform for quality local food, built on Locally4Me. *MVP 70%+.*
- **LETE — the Data Protection Protocol** — data-security software and privacy/data-protection layer. *Detailed blueprints, v1 ready for delegation.*

## Ways to get involved

Always open to discussing technical partnerships, research collaborations, pilot and implementation projects, consulting, grant initiatives, and sponsorship. If your organization, company, or institution sees a potential fit, get in touch.

Some projects are in development and benefit from early backers. If you'd like to accelerate development through pre-purchase agreements, sponsorship, infrastructure support, or strategic backing, reach out.

The goal is simple: practical tools and systems that improve resilience, privacy, community coordination, and long-term societal preparedness. If that vision resonates with you, your support, collaboration, or participation is genuinely valued.
