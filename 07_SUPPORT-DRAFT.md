                                        # Essential Crisis Software (ECS)

## Open-Source Infrastructure for Crisis Resilience

Essential Crisis Software (ECS) is an open-source ecosystem of communication, coordination and continuity tools designed for situations where conventional infrastructure becomes unavailable, degraded or unreliable.

The project focuses on practical resilience rather than theoretical preparedness.

Potential scenarios include:

* Large-scale power outages
* Telecommunications failures
* Internet disruptions
* Natural disasters
* Supply chain breakdowns
* Humanitarian emergencies
* Wartime and hybrid-threat environments
* Community self-organization during crises

ECS aims to provide modular building blocks that help communities, organizations and local networks maintain situational awareness, communication and coordination when normal systems fail.

## Core Principles

* Open Source
* Privacy First
* Resilience by Design
* Community Ownership
* Offline-Capable Architecture
* Vendor Independence
* Ethical Technology

## Vision

The long-term vision of ECS is to become a practical resilience ecosystem that can be adapted by communities, municipalities, NGOs, emergency groups, researchers and citizens worldwide.

Rather than replacing existing systems, ECS aims to provide an additional layer of continuity and preparedness.
